About Me

Hello everybody! My name is Chao Wang. As of now, I hold the position of an assistant professor at the China Academy of Railway Sciences. My academic journey began with a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Jiangnan University in 2007, followed by a Master's in Computer Science from Northeastern University in 2010.

As a researcher, I am passionate about exploring cutting-edge technologies that can revolutionize the transportation industry. My research interests are diverse and include the development of intelligent rail transit systems, big data distributed processing systems, edge computing, graph neural networks, convolutional neural networks, object classification/detection, and the intersection of computer vision with interdisciplinary applications.

I have secured eight Chinese invention patents and three Chinese utility model patents, as well as published seven papers in peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings in my field.

My expertise in practical engineering projects has allowed me to lead and contribute to the research and development of numerous subway and high-speed rail projects in China. These projects have been successfully implemented and have yielded significant economic and social benefits for the country.

In the Beijing Rail Transit Seminar 2021, the team I led won the second prize in the intelligent test and verification platform project of an urban rail train control system based on private cloud architecture.

In 2020, I participated in a project on high redundancy fault safety computer technology and its application based on multi-mode information interaction and won the first prize in the China Academy of Railway Sciences.

In 2018, the intelligent software speed measurement system under my leadership won the Innovation Award of the Signal Institute of China Academy of Railway Sciences.

In 2018, I participated in a patent of an ATO speed measurement and ranging system, which won the China Excellent Patent Award.
